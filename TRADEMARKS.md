# Tiger Trademark Policy

**Short version:** the *code* is open (BSD-3-Clause — do almost anything with it). The
*names and logos* are not. This keeps "Tiger" meaning the real thing, so nobody gets
confused by a fork or a knock-off wearing our name.

## The marks

"**Tiger**", "**TigerZF**", "**TigerDocs**", and "**WebTigers**", together with the Tiger
and WebTigers logos, are trademarks of **WebTigers** ("we", "us"). They are **not** covered
by the software license — [LICENSE](LICENSE) grants copyright permissions only, never
trademark rights.

## What you CAN do (no permission needed)

- Use, run, modify, fork, and redistribute the software under the BSD-3-Clause license.
- **Say what your software is truthfully** — "built on Tiger", "a Tiger module", "compatible
  with Tiger", "powered by TigerZF". Nominative, factual references are fine.
- Keep the existing trademark/copyright notices intact in the files you redistribute.
- Publish a module or theme in the community registry and describe accurately which Tiger
  version it targets.

## What you may NOT do (without prior written permission)

- **Name your fork, product, company, or service "Tiger", "WebTigers", or anything
  confusingly similar** (e.g. "Tiger Pro", "TigerCMS", "Tigerly"). Pick your own name; you
  can note it's *based on Tiger*.
- Use the names or logos in a way that **implies endorsement, affiliation, or sponsorship**
  by WebTigers when there is none.
- Use the logos in a modified form, or as your own app/product icon.
- Register the marks (or confusingly similar ones) as trademarks, domain names, social
  handles, or package names.

## Modules & the registry

Third-party modules are encouraged — that's the point of the open registry. Name your module
after **your** brand, not ours. "*Acme Billing for Tiger*" ✅. "*Tiger Billing*" ❌. A listing
that misuses the marks or implies official status may be declined or removed.

## Forks

You're free to fork. If you distribute a modified version, please **rename it** so users know
it isn't the official Tiger, and remove our logos from your distribution's branding (the
in-file trademark notices stay).

## Questions / permission

For any use beyond the above — partnerships, co-branding, anything you're unsure about —
reach out to **support@webtigers.com**. We're friendly; we just have to defend the mark to
keep it.
